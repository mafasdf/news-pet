INDONESIAN RUPIAH SLIPS AGAINST MARK AND YEN The Indonesian rupiah has held steadysince its 31 pct devaluation against the dollar six months ago,but has slipped against the mark and to a lesser extent againstthe yen, according to central bank figures.    In the past month, the rupiah has fallen five pct againstthe yen. Today's middle rate per 100 yen was 1,129.78 against1,075.20 at end-February and 1,058.6 at devaluation inSeptember.    Bank Indonesia's quoted rate for the dollar, the maincurrency for Indonesia's oil and gas exports, was 1,644.0today, the same rate fixed at the time of devaluation.    The rate for the West German mark was 913.28 today, a sharpdrop from September when it was 786.06.    The British pound has risen to 2,657.93 against 2,429.83.   The value of the rupiah is set daily against a basket ofcurrencies by the central bank.    The rise in the value of the mark and the yen has hitIndonesia by increasing its debt servicing levels. Its totaldisbursed foreign debt is estimated by the World Bank at 37billion dlrs.    Japan is one of Indonesia's key trading partners, takinghalf its oil exports. REUTER
