MAURITIUS LIFTS DUTY ON TEXTILE-RELATED GOODS Mauritius has lifted import duties ontextiles and other goods connected with the textile industry aspart of its economic liberalisation programme, Finance MinisterVishnu Lutchmeenaraido said.    Lutchmeenaraido said the move was aimed at boosting thelocal textiles industry and making this Indian Ocean island ashopping paradise for textile goods. He said all types oftextiles, textile machinery and associated goods such as sewingthread, buttons and collar supports would be placed on theduty-free list, effective from April 1.    Official sources said the government was also planning toabolish import duties on fertiliser and agricultural equipment,with the liberalisation measures would be extended to otherareas of industry later.    The textiles industry dominates Mauritius's ExportProcessing Zone, and the island has become a major exporter ofwoollen knitwear. Reuter
